Two-Factor Authentication A factor is a piece of information used to verify a person's identity for security purposes. The three most commonly recognized factors are:

  • 'Something you KNOW', such as a password or PIN
  • 'Something you HAVE', such as a credit card or mobile phone
  • 'Something you ARE', biometric data, such as fingerprint, retina

In today's increased mobile users, traditional user login method of simple username and password is not secure enough anymore and poses high risk to corporate data. Two-factor authentication reduces such risk by introducing the dynamic password generated using OATH (Open Authentication) event-based and time based algorithms.
